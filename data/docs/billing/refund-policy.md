# Refund Policy

This policy describes eligibility for refunds, the refund process, and timelines for all subscription plans.

## Eligibility

Refund eligibility depends on your subscription type and timing:

- **Monthly plans:** Eligible for a full refund if requested within 7 days of the billing date. After 7 days, no refund is issued, but you retain access through the end of the billing period.
- **Annual plans:** Eligible for a prorated refund within the first 30 days. After 30 days, refunds are not available, but you may downgrade to a free tier at any time.
- **Enterprise contracts:** Refund terms are governed by individual agreements. Contact your account manager for details.

Free-tier users are not eligible for refunds as no charges are incurred.

## How to Request a Refund

1. Log in to your account and navigate to Settings → Billing → Request Refund.
2. Select the charge you wish to dispute.
3. Provide a brief reason for the refund request.
4. Submit the request. You will receive a confirmation email within 1 business day.

Alternatively, contact billing support at billing@example.com with your account email and the charge date.

## Processing Timeline

- Approved refunds are processed within 5-10 business days.
- Refunds are issued to the original payment method.
- If the original payment method is no longer valid, we will contact you to arrange an alternative.

## Chargebacks

If you initiate a chargeback through your bank or credit card company instead of using our refund process, your account will be suspended pending investigation. We strongly recommend using the in-app refund process first, as chargebacks may result in permanent account restrictions.

## Exceptions

We may grant exceptions to this policy on a case-by-case basis for:

- Service outages exceeding 24 hours that materially impacted your usage.
- Billing errors caused by system malfunction.
- Documented accessibility issues that prevented use of the service.

To request an exception, email billing@example.com with subject line "Refund Exception Request" and include relevant documentation.
